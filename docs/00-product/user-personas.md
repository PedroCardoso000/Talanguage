# User Personas — Talanguage

## Purpose

This document defines the initial user profiles that guide product decisions, UX flows, feature prioritization, and AI-generated implementation choices.

Personas must be used to avoid building generic language-learning features that do not serve a clear user need.

## Primary Persona — Consistent Adult Learner

### Profile

An adult learner who wants to improve English or Spanish but struggles with consistency, practice, and knowing what to study next.

### Goals

- Build a daily practice routine;
- improve speaking confidence;
- improve writing accuracy;
- review vocabulary consistently;
- see visible progress;
- avoid switching between many tools.

### Pain Points

- Studies passively but does not practice enough;
- uses several disconnected tools;
- does not know if progress is happening;
- forgets words and expressions;
- lacks speaking opportunities;
- loses motivation because the learning path feels unclear.

### Product Needs

- Clear daily next action;
- simple practice modules;
- feedback after practice;
- review system;
- visible progress;
- low-friction user experience.

## Secondary Persona — Busy Professional

### Profile

A professional who needs English or Spanish for work, interviews, meetings, travel, or career growth.

### Goals

- Practice realistic communication scenarios;
- improve confidence in professional contexts;
- learn useful phrases;
- track improvement;
- maintain short and consistent sessions.

### Pain Points

- Has little time;
- does not want childish gamification;
- needs practical language use;
- wants measurable progress;
- needs focused exercises instead of broad lessons.

### Product Needs

- Short practice sessions;
- topic-based speaking and writing;
- useful vocabulary review;
- progress by skill;
- adult and professional tone.

## Secondary Persona — Self-Taught Learner

### Profile

A learner who already uses free or low-cost tools but lacks structure and continuity.

### Goals

- Organize scattered learning activities;
- practice more actively;
- receive correction;
- maintain flashcards;
- track daily performance.

### Pain Points

- Tool fragmentation;
- no unified progress history;
- too much passive content consumption;
- weak feedback loop;
- inconsistent review.

### Product Needs

- Integrated practice system;
- clear learning loop;
- simple dashboard;
- structured review;
- guided repetition.

## Non-Primary Personas

The first version must not optimize primarily for:

- children;
- schools;
- teachers managing classes;
- enterprise training managers;
- language influencers;
- content creators;
- certification candidates requiring formal exam preparation.

These users may be considered later, but they must not drive first-version product decisions.

## UX Implications

The product must be:

- adult;
- direct;
- clean;
- practical;
- low-friction;
- focused on action;
- not childish;
- not overloaded with content.
